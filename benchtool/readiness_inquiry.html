Burden of Proof

If there is belated or missing disclosure, People must establish they exercised (1) due diligence and (2) made reasonable inquiries to ascertain the existence of material and information subject to discovery prior to filing a Certificate of Compliance (COC).1

Post-COC disclosure and a Supplemental COC cannot cure lack of due diligence prior to COC.2

Good faith also not enough to cure lack of diligence prior to COC.3

Due Diligence Factors

Due diligence factors, among other things:4

  • Efforts made by People to comply with CPL 245.20
  • Volume of discovery provided and outstanding
  • Complexity of the case
  • How obvious any missing material would have been if due diligence was exercised.
  • Explanation for discovery lapse
  • The People's response when apprised of any missing discovery

Supplemental COC must detail the basis for delayed disclosure so Court can determine if original COC is proper.5

Discovery sanction for belated disclosure only if COC is proper.6

Court Inquiry

Inquire of the People, allow Defense to respond:

  • Has a COC been filed?
  • Are you, or another prosecutor, presently available to try the case?
  • Are your witnesses presently available to testify?
  • Any expert testimony expected? If so, is expert presently available to testify?
  • Have all exhibits to be used at trial been turned over?
  • Have the People done all required to make the case trial ready?

Conclusion on the Record

__ I find the COC is proper and the People's Statement of Readiness is valid.

__ I find the COC is improper and the People's Statement of Readiness is stricken.

Note: Defendant may make motion to dismiss (CPL 30.30[1]) in writing OR motion to release (CPL 30.30[2]) orally or in writing if prosecution is untimely.

Motion to Dismiss (CPL 30.30[1])

People must be Ready for Trial where highest charge is:

  • Non-homicide Felony: 6 months
  • Misd. (>3 mos. Jail): 90 days
  • Misd. (<3 mos. Jail): 60 days
  • Violation: 30 days

Motion to Release (CPL 30.30[2])

People must be Ready for Trial where highest charge is:

  • Non-homicide Felony: 3 months
  • Misd. (>3 mos. Jail): 30 days
  • Misd. (<3 mos. Jail): 15 days
  • Violation: 5 days
